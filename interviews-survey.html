The interview shelf

What the doc_kinds=["interview"] filter isolates: five publication-style documents — edited for print, unlike the raw lecture transcripts — holding biographical facts recorded nowhere else in the corpus.

search(query="daily life work family marriage",
       doc_kinds=["interview"], k=20)
→ 5 hits — the entire interview collection; each document is one long exchange, read in full for this survey.

Inventory

The shelf — five documents

All from SOURCES-Dubai/…/ENGLISH TEXTS/INTERVIEWS/, all PDF-derived, each a single exchange spanning the whole piece.

  • “My Master's Teaching — I Am Only the Speaker” richest

    A long Bombay sitting with the interviewer Madhukar (before a France trip — “Why don't you accompany me to France next week?”). Doctrine (free will inside dreams, brahma vs. Parabrahma via the cinema screen) plus the corpus's most concentrated run of biographical fact: mastership in 1983, the mantra story, Siddharameshwar's daily discourses and death. OCR is rough — speaker labels garbled throughout.

    ranjit/interviews/iamonlythespeaker#x0000 · pp. 1–13 · IamOnlyTheSpeaker.pdf

  • Interview by Madhukar, Germany 1996 edited

    A polished, print-ready edit of Madhukar interview material: may a disciple declare enlightenment, can a guru give it, the “dry leaf in the wind”, and a three-way conversation with Sunderam, the attendant of the recently deceased Annamalai Swami — Maharaj interviews him.

    ranjit/interviews/rminterview-germany1996-from-manfred#x0000 · pp. 1–10 · RMInterview-Germany1996-from-Manfred.pdf

  • “The Realized-One: He Was Never Born — How Can He Die?” variant

    A rawer variant of the same Madhukar session — same questions, same Sunderam conversation, different editing. Useful for seeing what the polished version smoothed over.

    ranjit/interviews/neverborn#x0000 · pp. 1–6 · NeverBorn.pdf

  • “Meeting Ranjit Maharaj” — Inner Directions Journal compiled

    Compiled by Anne Maroger and Darrell Newberg from three days in Brittany, June 1996. Thematic selections (Reality & methods, knowledge & ignorance, the Master, death & fear) with the only editorial framing of the lineage in the corpus: the Navnath Sampradaya introduction placing Ranjit beside Nisargadatta.

    ranjit/interviews/meetingranjitmaharaj#x0000 · pp. 1–3 · MeetingRanjitMaharaj.pdf

  • Germany 1998 — the Krahberg interview duplicate

    Ottfried's Schloss Krahberg interview: the fullest Siddharameshwar biography plus the creativity / vidya-maya question series. The same text is filed in the corpus twice — here and as the lecture transcript 1998-06-krahberg-interview.

    ranjit/interviews/rminterview-germany-1998#x0000 · RMInterview-Germany-1998.pdf

Findings

What only the interviews record

Biographical facts that the lecture transcripts touch only glancingly, if at all.

  • His mastership began in 1983 — forty-seven years after Siddharameshwar's death — and he never sought it: “I never wanted to become a master.” iamonlythespeaker
  • Between 1936 and 1983 he “was living my own life” — no discourses, no disciples — though he lectured at his Master's birthday and mahasamadhi anniversaries together with Nisargadatta. iamonlythespeaker
  • Siddharameshwar gave one discourse a day to 200–300 people, spoke at no other time, and taught for ten years before dying at 48. iamonlythespeaker
  • The cause and date of Siddharameshwar's death: untreated chronic diabetes, boils, a proposed operation — 14 November 1936, two and a half months after the boils appeared. iamonlythespeaker
  • Siddharameshwar had a wife and children and renounced the world at 28. iamonlythespeaker
  • The teaching's provenance, in one line: “His knowledge I am conveying to you. It is not my knowledge. I am just the speaker.” iamonlythespeaker
  • The reversal of tradition that defines the lineage's pedagogy: Siddharameshwar gave knowledge first, holding that renunciation without knowledge is “a bondage of the mind” — the poisoned-meal example. meetingranjitmaharaj

Verbatim

Four passages

  • How the mastership began — the mantra story

    My guruji’s grandson’s wife came to me in 1983 and asked me to give her a mantra. I had to give it to her. Because if | wouldn’t have given it to her, I would have been fatalistic towards my master. With that incident my mastership started. From then on I started to have disciples.

    At that time it was a really great question for me whether to give her the mantra or not. The question was to accept mastership or not. But I didn’t have any other choice really because I felt I couldn’t refuse the mantra to my guru’s grandson’s wife. That occasion converted my life info a life of mastership.

    ranjit/interviews/iamonlythespeaker#x0000 (OCR artifacts preserved)

  • Siddharameshwar's own words — the twenty-three hours

    My teacher used to tell me, “There is only One. Every day I step down from my Self for one hour to give a discourse. During this time, I take you as my disciples and I am your guru. I am your teacher and I teach you. The other twenty-three hours of my daily life, | am in my Self.”

    ranjit/interviews/rminterview-germany1996-from-manfred#x0000

  • The test of a real master

    The master should be alive. The real master initiates an immediate change in a seeker’s life. If there is no change, the master is not real. The change must be obvious and noticeable.

    ranjit/interviews/rminterview-germany1996-from-manfred#x0000

  • The dry leaf

    Everything is part of the dream including the seeker and his practice. By hearing the facts, the seeker will realize that he is part of the dream—nothing else. Practice is only necessary up to that realization. Once that realization occurs, nothing remains to be done. An understanding person goes on to simply live his life. He lives like a dry leaf in the wind. Without desire or volition, the leaf is carried by the wind in all directions. Such a person has nothing to worry about.

    ranjit/interviews/rminterview-germany1996-from-manfred#x0000

Notes

Notes for the curator

  • Two variants of one session. neverborn and rminterview-germany1996-from-manfred carry the same Madhukar interview material (identical question sequence and the Sunderam conversation) in raw and edited form — worth flagging if the corpus later wants a canonical-version policy for published pieces.
  • One cross-kind duplicate. rminterview-germany-1998 (interview) and 1998-06-krahberg-interview (transcript) are the same text under two doc kinds; searches ranking both will double-count it.
  • Garbled speaker labels. The iamonlythespeaker OCR interleaves runs of “V(M): RM: V(M):” detached from their turns — the words are readable but turn attribution needs care when quoting dialogue from it.
  • Weak titles. Extracted titles for three of the five (“RM:”, “—”, “Ranjit Maharaj”) come from OCR headers, not the pieces' actual names.
  • The interviewer matters. These are the only corpus documents where the questioner is a writer working toward publication (Madhukar; Inner Directions Journal) — the prose is polished, and editorial framing (the Navnath Sampradaya introduction) is mixed with the Master's words. The effective-text discipline of the transcripts applies, but here the editor's hand predates the corpus.
