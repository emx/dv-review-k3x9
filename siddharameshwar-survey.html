Maharaj on Siddharameshwar

Every reference in the corpus where Shri Ranjit Maharaj speaks of his Master, Shri Siddharameshwar Maharaj — summarized in context with dates, places, and citations, grouped by theme. Companion to the Maharaj on the Dasbodh survey.

Method & scope. Found by three passes — a lexical search for Siddharameshwar (curated variant expansion also matched Siddharameshvar, Siddharameswar), a semantic pass, and a lexical pass on my Master said — with the eight most substantive exchanges read in full. Entries are context summaries, not transcriptions; the short fragments in italics are verbatim. Ritual invocations opening the translated pravachans are noted once under incidental mentions.

Theme 1

Biography & lineage

  • The fullest account of His life

    Asked to introduce his Master, Maharaj gives the whole arc: “the Greatest Saint of the time… unknown, because He never wanted fame or name” — initiated by Bhausaheb Maharaj in 1906; Bhausaheb dies 1914; Siddharameshwar takes the Mastership in 1918 and tours India teaching meditation; asks his co-disciples to go beyond meditation and they refuse; withdraws to Bijapur and meditates nine months without stopping; discovers the vihangam maarga (the bird's way — realization by thinking rather than long meditation); teaches from 1924, gives the complete understanding within twelve years, leaves the body at 48. Ends: “At the age of 12, I was initiated by Him, in 1925.”

    Krahberg interview, Jun 1998 · pp. 1–6 · ranjit/english-lectures/1998-06-krahberg-interview#x0000 (same text filed as ranjit/interviews/rminterview-germany-1998#x0000)

  • Lineage recitation

    “My Master's name was Siddharameshwar Maharaj. His Master was Bhausaheb Maharaj. His Master was Guru-linga-jangam. They were from Karnataka, both.”

    Hawaii, 18 Jun 1999 · ranjit/english-lectures/1999-06-18-hawaii#x0002, #x0005

  • The ashram story — counting the stanzas

    Accused of sleeping during the sacred-book reading in Bhausaheb Maharaj's ashram, Siddharameshwar retorts that the reader is on the 12th stanza; rebuked by Bhausaheb for refusing the order, He leaves for Karnataka and sits nine months “to find out the easiest way.”

    Hawaii, 18 Jun 1999 · pp. 13–16 · ranjit/english-lectures/1999-06-18-hawaii#x0012

  • His age

    Asked how long Siddharameshwar Maharaj lived: “49 only. 48 ...”

    7 Sep 1998 · ranjit/english-lectures/1998-09-07#x0087

  • “I got Him at the age of 12”

    A rare personal aside — three brothers and one sister, all married into worldly life, “I got Him (Siddharameshwar Maharaj) at the age of 12, when I was 12 years old.”

    23 Feb 1998 · p. 60 · ranjit/english-lectures/1998-02-23#x0050

  • The Nisargadatta connection

    Confirms Siddharameshwar is “also Master of Nisargadatta” and explains that Maharaj is a title, the name is Siddharameshwar; again in 1998, answering “HE's the same Master from Nisargadatta?”; and pointing to the photo: “He was my co-disciple… our Master was He.”

    14 Jan 1997 · ranjit/english-lectures/1997-01-14#x0072–73 · 5 Mar 1998 · 1998-03-05#x0075–76 · San Francisco, 20 Apr 1998 · 1998-04-20pm-sanfrancisco#x0016

Theme 2

How He taught

  • Simplicity as method

    “Greatest Saint of the age. But He was unknown.” Spoke only Marathi, Gujarati, and Kannada — no English; taught women and children against the orthodox bar of the day; Maharaj's own illiterate mother came daily to hear Him; and He “had the greatest understanding of breaking the words” — the vrutti / varti wordplay as an example of how He opened meanings.

    San Francisco, 24 May 1997 · pp. 1–5 · ranjit/english-lectures/1997-05-24pm-sanfrancisco#x0001

  • “Be out of the circle”

    He taught in a very simple way, unlike the masters who keep you moving inside the circle; “One who says you are Perfect, nothing to do, he's the Real Master.”

    19 Dec 1996 · pp. 57–58 · ranjit/english-lectures/1996-12-19-lecture2#x0097

  • The four books

    Evening lectures on the Dasbodh in Bombay for 1½–2 months at a stretch (likewise Solapur), bhajan following at night; also taught from Yoga Vasistha, Sadachar, and Eknathi Bhagwat — “four books He took to teach us”; His motto that “high bombastic words are not required.”

    Brittany interview, 8 Jun 1996 · pp. 6–8 · ranjit/english-lectures/1996-06-08-brittany-interview#x0005

  • “He gave out the real thing”

    Contrasted with the many saints who put aspirants on the wrong things: “my Master, Siddharameshwar Maharaj, He gave out that, the real thing. And He was so competent in His mind, that He can teach anybody at any time.”

    Krahberg, 10 Jun 1998 · ranjit/english-lectures/1998-06-10-krahberg#x0000

  • The Dasbodh inheritance

    The lineage scripture was “a book followed by my Master's Master. And I take it granted and true, because my Master showed the meaning of it, from there.”

    Sedona, 8 May 1998 · pp. 30–33 · ranjit/english-lectures/1998-05-08am-sedona#x0022

Theme 3

Beyond meditation

  • The easier way

    His Master gave the understanding of meditation, but He said “No, something can be, we have to go by the easier way” — and found, from meditation itself, that by thinking alone one can be He.

    Brittany, 2 May 1999 · ranjit/english-lectures/1999-05-02-brittany#x0011

  • Personal guidance

    Asked how he knew it was time to discontinue his own meditation: “My Master told me. He always guides…”

    San Francisco, 21 May 1997 · ranjit/english-lectures/1997-05-21am-sanfrancisco#x0022

  • “Either I die or I achieve my goal”

    The 1979 Marathi account (translated) of His resolve after the co-disciples refused to go beyond Bhausaheb's instruction: He put in tremendous effort, His body “became like a stick for the sake of that knowledge.”

    Bhausaheb Maharaj utsav talk, 1979 (translation) · ranjit/translations/bmju-1979-translation#t0021–22

Theme 4

After His passing, and the Power

  • “You and He become One”

    Asked about his relationship with Siddharameshwar since He left the body: “After death, relationship doesn't remain… he and He, means Master, they become One.” Recounts that in 1935 He said “I give my Power to you. One who will understand and use that Power… He will be the Greatest of the Greatest!” — followed by the Akbar-and-Birbal gallows story and the warning against the ego of the “oldest disciple” (patta-shishya).

    Brittany, 21 Jun 2000 · pp. 1–7 · ranjit/english-lectures/2000-06-21-brittany#x0001

  • When He disappeared

    A questioner asks whether the picture — the Power appearing spontaneously — stopped when Siddharameshwar disappeared; taken up in the following exchange.

    22 Sep 1998 · p. 39 · ranjit/english-lectures/1998-09-22#x0039–40

  • Sixty years on — and His memorials

    A 1997 Guru Purnima pravachan (translated) opens by noting sixty years since (Siddharameshwar) Maharaj left the body. The Pathri 1998 saptah opens remembering His son, Motiram Maharaj, and a note records the Bagewadi linga replaced by Siddharameshwar's statue.

    ranjit/translations/gurupurnima-d0101-1997-07-18-translation#t0002 · ranjit/translations/pathri-d0101-01feb1998-translation-…#t0002, #t0009

Theme 5

Stories he tells about Him

  • “God is first or space is first?”

    Visiting another saint with eighteen fellow disciples (the host orders eighteen teas), Siddharameshwar asks whether God or space is first; when the saint answers “God is first,” He corrects him from Sadachar — whoever makes the space is not true either.

    17 Mar 1998 · pp. 66–68 · ranjit/english-lectures/1998-03-17#x0061–65

  • “Your Shankaracharya…”

    His Master overrules an appeal to Shankaracharya's authority in an exchange about who or what is God.

    19 Feb 1997 · ranjit/english-lectures/1997-02-19#x0099

  • A disciple's plea

    “Siddharameshwar Maharaj gave this example. A disciple came, ‘Maharaj I have come…’” — an example He used, retold in a 1999 guruseva talk.

    Guruseva talk, 1999 (translation) · ranjit/translations/guruseva-s-15-1999-translation#t0032

  • The Diwali Saptah

    He conducted the Saptah after Bhausaheb Maharaj's departure — the 1979 Diwali talk notes the programme celebrated by Him since 1918; the 1994 Guru Purnima talk recalls where He used to conduct it.

    ranjit/translations/d0101-1979-diwalitranslation#t0002, #t0117 · ranjit/translations/gp-1003-1994-translation#t0029

Theme 6

His books, invoked in answers

  • Amrut Laya

    Quoted back and forth across the talks: “Siddharameshwar says, in Amrut Laya He said, only swimmers can cross” (Brittany 2000); a questioner cites it in Paris the same year; and “That is the sermons of Siddharameshwar Maharaj” identifying the book's contents.

    Brittany, 21 Jun 2000 · 2000-06-21-brittany#x0005 · Paris, 31 May 2000 · 2000-05-31-paris#x0004 · 23 Sep 1998 · 1998-09-23#x0002

  • Master Key to Self Realization

    Recommended among “the books of Siddharameshwar Maharaj's teaching”; a disciple mentions having read and typed it.

    Brittany, 27 May 1998 · 1998-05-27-brittany#x0000 · Jan 1996 · 1996-01-lecture2#x0012

  • “Stateless State”

    A questioner names it as the state “that Your Master, Siddharameshwar Maharaj, talks about”; Maharaj: “Correct.” Elsewhere: “Siddharameshwar Maharaj also is talking in the pravachan…”

    Paris, 30 May 2000 · 2000-05-30-paris#x0002 · 1994 · 1994-lecture2#x0020
