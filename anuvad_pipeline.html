२ · why this is hard

The dangerous errors are invisible

The source PDF is born-digital, but its text layer is mojibake from a broken legacy font — unusable. So the pages are treated as images: rasterized and read by OCR. That opens the real problem:

An OCR mistake in Devanāgarī usually produces valid-looking Devanāgarī. धम्म is a perfectly renderable word — it's simply not the word on the page. No mechanical check can catch it, no non-reader can see it, and in a revered religious text a plausible-but-wrong "correction" is worse than the error it replaced. The project calls this the cardinal sin: silent corruption.

Every design choice below is downstream of that one fact. The pipeline is not built to fix everything; it is built so that everything it cannot prove, it marks — ⟦?⟧ — and everything it ships unmarked, it can defend with evidence.

३ · the pipeline, stage by stage

From broken PDF to honest book

Nine stages. Each card: what enters, what leaves, what guards it, and the register decisions that govern it. Indigo chips mark machinery that was built, measured, and retired to telemetry — kept as evidence, forbidden from touching text.

०SOURCE

Rasterize stable

The mojibake text layer is discarded entirely. Pages become images at 192 DPI — measured as the sweet spot for VLM OCR on this font. Higher resolution hurts (the model downsamples to a fixed token budget). Classical "300 DPI" folklore does not apply and was tested, not assumed.

in Dāsbodh PDF, 796 pp  ·  out page rasters @192

१OCR

Two independent readers stable

Surya 2 (VLM OCR, primary) and Google Vision (second engine) each read every page, run out-of-band by the operator. Two engines exist for one reason: agreement is confidence, disagreement is exactly where to look.

in rasters  ·  out two full independent readings per page

Extract into units stable

Surya's output is parsed into the book's own anatomy: ovī (verse) · commentary (prose) · heading · śloka, with dashak/samās structure derived from page footers. The unit is the atom everything downstream operates on — 16,170 of them.

in OCR JSON  ·  out typed, ordered units

३CONSENSUS

Engines vote, per occurrence stable

Every reading site lands in one of three buckets. Unanimous — engines agree: the text stands. Flag — genuine uncertainty: the site gets ⟦?⟧ candidates. ocr-split — engines disagree at input level: the Surya baseline stands (policy decided on evidence: the reader sided with Surya 9–1 in a blind probe; Vision's failure mode named).

Alongside runs the glyphbreak axis — forensics on the broken legacy font's glyph shapes. It informs which sites are suspicious. It is telemetry.

in two readings  ·  out unanimous text + flagged sites + split log
D-013 D-023 R-C
४CANON

Canonical alignment — the verse goes gold stable

The Dāsbodh's verse exists as a canonical edition (Marathi Wikisource). Every OCR'd ovī is aligned against it: a confident match ships the canonical text — the strongest evidence in the whole pipeline. No confident match → the OCR text ships with a unit-level flag, never guessed, never partial-aligned. Result: 7,726 of 7,743 ovīs (99.78%) confidently canonical; 186 unit-flagged; 0 cardinal-sin violations.

Caveat, standing: canonical is a different edition — orthographic variants are not OCR errors. Strong for verse; the commentary has no canonical source and never touches this stage.

in verse units  ·  out gold verse / flagged verse

५READER

Human evidence — the ruling cache text-application retired

Vidya Padvi is the sole content authority — the only party who reads the script. Four sessions (D3 review · intra-rater A, 8/10 · accuracy B · template D4) produced 47 byte-rulings in a cache. The critical history: every attempt to convert her identity rulings ("this glyph is X") into automatic text substitution was measured and corrupted words. All three categories — class, name, byte-constant — are retired to telemetry.

Final cache census: 1 signal · 35 telemetry · 11 anti · 0 constant — no byte changes any text. Her rulings remain fully valid as glyph-identity evidence; the byte-wide application was the defect, not her reading.

  • Reader criterion: a question reaches her only if the answer changes a rule, a policy, or a release decision — never to settle one occurrence.
  • Reader pages ship under a 4-item checklist (cold presentation, foreign-state guard, storage-state runtime checks, on-device dry run).
in ambiguous glyph classes  ·  out identity evidence (telemetry) + anti-rules
D-016…D-022 D-025 D-032
६MATERIALIZE

Place the flags — change nothing stable

Per dashak, the commentary text is finalized: unresolved uncertainty sites receive their visible ⟦?⟧ markers. Under the completed doctrine this stage applies zero substitutions — it is the abstention made concrete. It is also the most heavily guarded step in the pipeline:

  • Rewrite guard — content-substitution units ≤ 20% of a dashak, and ≤ 400 occurrences. Threat: unreviewed text change.
  • Flag guard — ⟦?⟧-marker units ≤ 60%. Threat: upstream degradation. Separate from the rewrite guard by design: a guard must never punish abstention.
  • Verse-skip guard — edits targeting verse abort the dashak if count > 5 and rate > 3% (verse is gold; the materializer must never touch it).
  • Abort hygiene — outputs invalidated up front, written via temp + atomic replace: a tripped guard can never leave a stale artifact looking current.
  • Linguistic sanity — mandatory diff-sample after any pass with substitutions > 0 (reph-loss / conjunct-break / canon-unattested). Retro-tested: it catches the v1.0 corruption 442/637.
  • Anti-loop rule — a tripped dashak ships OCR baseline + flags, logged, not debugged. Not-correcting is always a valid ship state. D2 and D17 shipped exactly this way.
in units + cache + guards  ·  out final per-dashak text, deterministic

Adversaries all the way down QA in flight

४ · the rules — set as the book itself is set: rule, then commentary

The doctrine

Ten standing rules. None was designed in advance; each was earned, and each cites the decision that made it law.

Demonstrate, don't assert.
Self-reports — the coding agent's, the AI's, any reviewer's — are inadmissible. Only on-disk artifacts, measured numbers, and demonstrated behavior count. This has repeatedly caught real bugs and one forced-fix attempt.
standing since inception · applied to every handback
Identity is telemetry; text needs per-occurrence evidence.
A ruling that a glyph, name, or byte "is X" never licenses substituting X into running text — the same byte is different glyphs in different words. The core lesson, paid for three times at three levels: class, name, byte.
D-022 · D-025 · D-032
Never silently correct — flag.
An invisible wrong word in a sacred text is worse than a visible open question. ⟦?⟧ is not a defect ledger; it is the product working. The project is explicitly not chasing 100%.
founding principle · D-021 made it measurable
Traceable-and-deterministic is not correct.
v1.0's corruption had perfect provenance and perfect reproducibility — and 442 wrong words. Auditability is necessary, never sufficient; hence the mandatory linguistic-sanity stage on any text-changing pass.
D-032 R2 · measured a second time by D-035
One configuration, one run; a near-miss is a miss.
Gates are pre-committed before the measurement, then read literally. Peeking at failures and re-running measures the patch, not the system. USV came in at 98.76% against 99% and died — that is the rule holding, not failing.
D-029 · executed by D-035
Every failure path terminates in a ship state.
A tripped guard means "don't correct here" — the dashak ships baseline + flags, logged, never individually debugged. No iteration loops, no per-item chases; a systematic pattern (≥4 same-cause trips) is reported once, after the batch.
D-027 · exercised by D2 and D17
A guard must never punish abstention.
The change-rate guard is two independent gates — rewrite (≤20%) and flag-density (≤60%) — because a single gate conflating them would incentivize silent guessing, the one failure the project exists to prevent.
D-026
Ask the reader only what changes a rule, a policy, or a release.
Vidya's attention is the scarcest resource in the system. Never a per-occurrence question; sessions are budgeted, cold-presented, and shipped under the four-item page checklist.
D-027 · reader-page checklist after the Session-B incident
Build behind evidence.
No machinery is constructed ahead of the proof that justifies it. The corollary was learned at a stocktake: an eager correction layer generated most of its own workload.
standing · reaffirmed at the D-023 gate
The decision gates history, not the keyboard.
No commit or tag moves without a per-instance operator approval recorded in the conversation; the agent then executes exactly what was approved and reports the hash. Approvals are scoped to a shown diffstat — a changed diff voids the approval.

५ · how the rules were earned

Four corruptions, each caught earlier than the last

The same disease four times — an identity judgment acting as a text edit — attacked at four levels. Watch the catch-point move upstream. That movement is the pipeline maturing.

1 · Class rules corrupt prose D-021/D-022

Glyph-class rulings applied as substitutions produced bare-virama fragments and reph-loss doublings (सर्वत्र → सव्वत्र). Session B measured the flag-corrected stratum at 1/15 faithful. Response: the entire gb-signal category retired to telemetry — not a patched guard tuned to the sampled failures.

in the D4 template run

2 · Names injected into scripture D-024/D-025

A reader's identity label was promoted to a book-wide substitution: the literal string "Anusvara ◌ं" written inside Marathi words, and a sentinel that would have replaced a vowel-sign with a consonant 122 times. The guard aborted the dashak. Response: all naming/sentinel application retired; label-vs-derived input validation added.

ground-truth build

3 · Byte constants — the v1.0 corruption D-030/D-032

The last surviving category: byte-wide constants. 442 of 638 substitutions overwrote a genuine reph (धर्म → धम्म) — valid-looking, fully traceable, deterministic, wrong. v1.0 was rebuilt as v1.0.1 with zero substitutions; all 637 sites verified restored to baseline. The doctrine line completed: class, name, and byte.

a single unit, by a
pre-committed gate — $15

4 · The LLM oracle fails its exam D-035

Sentence-level LLM arbitration of flags — the most sophisticated corrector yet — was calibrated against 643 known answers behind a ≥99% gate written before the run. It scored 98.76%, and 7 of its 8 errors were the same reph class. Rejected for v1.x, permanently, no tuning. The flags ship as flags.

The trajectory to validate: shipped-then-reverted → blocked at the batch → blocked at the tag → blocked at a $15 calibration. Each guard added after an incident caught the next incident earlier. The pipeline's safety is not a claim; it is this recorded sequence.

७ · what is open — and what is deliberately closed

The honest remainder

in flight
Final QA session — 30 stratified units under Vidya's eye (seed 20260705). The one number: silent corruption N/30. Any hit is a STOP and a director decision, never a reflexive fix.
at the Phase-2 gate
Flag consumption — how the translator sees ⟦?⟧ sites: read through, abstain, or consult candidates from the audit layer. Decides whether two text-invariant input improvements (placing D2/D17's inventoried flags; snapping mid-cluster markers to akshara boundaries) earn a build.
at the Phase-2 gate
Sentence verification, redesigned — the measured 98.76% oracle plus hard mechanical guards (reph-loss is deterministically detectable) plus fresh ground truth it has never seen. Reserved by D-035 for translation-era stakes, where a wrong pick costs an English nuance, not a sacred word.
parked
Batch-API wiring & honest cost — ≈$2.8k at current settings, halvable; confirmed before any translation run.
documented debt
60 migration-gated tests — skip cleanly with reasons; suite is green-or-skipped, red means something. Real-fixture migration tracked, not urgent.
closed by doctrine
Everything else. The 2,805 glyph flags, the 186 unmatched ovīs, the 7 A2 units: each sits exactly where the evidence left it. Reopening any of them without new evidence is the loop, refused by design.
